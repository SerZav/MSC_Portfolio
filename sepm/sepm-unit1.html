Initial Post

To me, complexity is the main cause of most project failures. There is a range of related causes related to complexity, such as available resources., technological gaps between new systems and legacy systems that have been running for years or decades, bureaucracy built-in large government or industrial ecosystems, among others. When a system is very complex, extra measures are required to avoid failure. The expertise of project managers and developers, testers, architects, designers, and everyone involved in the SDLC, must be suited for the task. Documentation and communication between all parties involved play a significant role in the project success. As an example of failure due to project complexity, we have the Boeing 737 Max that resulted in two plane crashes and put the project on hold for investigations (Johnston & Harris, 2019).

For me, the second most important cause of project failures is related to people. It can be argued that there are many ramifications to this cause such as inexperienced developers and testers or poor management selection. This fact often weakens the foundations of the project. For example, we have the European Ariane 5 crash, 40 seconds after take-off (Jazequel & Meyer, 1997).

Another common cause of failures is related to estimation and analysis. Again, we found this cause closely related to others such as people (poor management) or project complexity, which can lead to errors in estimation without the right people for the job. One of the most common examples to showcase this failure was the development of OS/360 by IBM, where multiple estimation problems occurred and were subsequently discussed, and documented (Brooks, 1974).

About SEPM

Software Engineering Project Management requires knowledge of the phases involved in the Software Development Lifecycle, from the requirements to the deployment. Just as a traditional Project Management it includes recruiting the team members, gathering requirements and estimating delivery time, but it also includes design, programming and testing.

Software Engineering Project Manager must be prepared to gather business requirements, manage resources, get involved with stakeholders and deal with expectations of every party involved.

TOGAF Architecture Development Method

It is a method that describes a cyclical pattern where four main phases iterate over the requirements of the project. These phases are:

Business: involves the preliminary, business vision and business architecture phases. The PM work with stakeholders and business architects to produce the initial documentation, design, agreements, etc.

IT: involves infrastructure architects, developers, and analysts to decide on platforms, infrastructure, and software architecture.

Planning: the PM creates the plans for the migration or the new solution proposal, showcasing opportunities and solutions.

Change: involve the possible changes that arise after some challenges or issues are encountered. These changes may need to revise the initial design, by making small changes or even forcing a new design cycle.

Around 70% of the incidents are introduced in the initial phases. However, most incidents are identified in the final stages, which is more expensive for the project (Feiler, 2019).

Common causes of project failures

Organisation and planning (scheduling, procurement, etc).

Leadership and governance: team and contractor management, poor strategic vision, or deficient direction through the development cycle.

Estimation and analysis: communication problems, bad estimation, bad design, communication, etc.

Quality: lack of testing and delivery management.

Risk: lack of risk assessment and modelling, lack of suitable tools to measure risks.

Skills and competency: poor design, poor implementation or poor testing due to lack of knowledge or unexperienced personnel.

Communication and engagement: bad expectation management (i.e., unrealistic projections or expectations), stakeholder management (i.e., bad communication during initial phases).

Complexity: Unproven technologies used in complex systems, or integration with highly complex systems.

About project failures

According to Agarwal and Rathod (2006), it is almost impossible to agree whether a project was a success or a failure. It is common to see the scenario where developers think a project was a success while stakeholders think it was a complete failure.

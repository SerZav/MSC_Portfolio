Behaviour Driven development

It was born as an evolution of TDD where stakeholders are included in the equation, so they can test the solution without the need of being a developer. It is necessary to establish a strong communication and a clear understanding of the software. Test cases must be developed with that scenario in mind. It also includes features of DDD such as ubiquitous language, where a common non-technical language is used to describe the software characteristics, that can be understood by developers and stakeholders.

One characteristic of BDD is that normally the development starts from the GUI, so that customers can start collaborating and suggesting. The user’s interface becomes another stakeholder and can start interacting with other pieces of the code.

Artefacts

The seminar 1 preparation initiated an interesting debate on using Behaviour Driven Development techniques, such as Gherkin Language.

Gherkin Language

It could be argued that it would be easier to exercise as PM for the assignment during the first two weeks. But it was challenging to decide on tools and establish an initial dynamic for the group. Some were worried about a slow start, while others were disconnected from the team because the module was in an early stage. I was under the impression that many were thinking about catching up during the break, and I felt like it was a bad idea overall.

Setting goals for the following sessions has proven a challenging task. Bad planning of work ahead could affect the outcome of the assignment. Being a big team, there is a risk to put an extra burden on some participants.

In my opinion, it is not easy to become acquainted with many of the PM’s responsibilities in only two weeks. It was very interesting to test management tools and evaluate their different possibilities. I am sure it will be useful for every team member to experiment with one of these tools. According to our discussion, none of them has been PM before, so it is a good opportunity to test and put into practice some of the knowledge available for project management.

European Quality of Life Survey 2016.

I have often considered surveys uncomfortable, especially when they are very long or unsolicited. It is easy to see ourselves reflected as the target of a bad designed questionnaire. I have personally conducted a few surveys and designed some questionnaires without considering all the aspects that could affect its reliability.

While participating in different surveys I had the opportunity to experience good and bad designs, without critically evaluating the reasons behind their success or failure. Past experiences may have conditioned my acceptance of their importance or reliability. The tasks and papers discussed over this unit will help me to analyse questionnaires in a different way, allowing me to distinguish the pros and cons or the improvement areas. It will be challenging to develop this skill, which is hard to master but very rewarding.

European Quality of Life Survey 2016.

The European Quality of Life Survey questionnaire was developed by Eurofound, and it has been translated to every European country's language. It is focused on public services such as healthcare, education, facilities, information available, and other aspects of quality of life.

The format used is unique, with tailored questions for this specific goal. The questionnaire comprises 104 questions which makes it very long for most interviewees. The questions format is very similar, with small variations throughout the questionnaire. Most questions are closed, with a set of alternatives to choose. Respondents have the option to refuse answering or select others, which makes them feel they have control over the information they want to provide. I would change the format of the questions constantly so it would be more enjoyable for the respondents.

The approximate duration of the questionnaire is forty minutes. In order to improve its reliability, I would change the format of some questions or interpose the format every five questions to improve the respondent interest and thus, enhance its focus and disposition to answer.

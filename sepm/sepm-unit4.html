About project constraints

A project is normally constrained by time, scope and cost. These are referred to as “triple constraint”. Time indicates the project duration or how is it projected over a specific schedule. Scope defines the extend of responsibilities to accomplish during the project or the specific tasks that will be carried out. Cost involves the budged available to spend on resources to complete the project (Augsburg Education, 2012).

Sounds Of New York City (SONYC) project was founded to build and deploy noise sensors, working in collaboration with the NYC Department of Environmental Protection. It involves large-scale noise monitoring, machine learning technology, big data analysis, and citizen science reporting to more effectively monitor, analyse, and respond to urban noise pollution.

Functional Requirements:

Store 24 hours of noise information levels: it is important to keep all data to successfully evaluate the available information during the whole day.

Connect wirelessly to the network: cables should be avoided in order to diminish disruption or disturbance to the general population.

Use solar powered devices only: solar power can help avoiding cables and save on electricity costs.

Look and feel requirements (Non-functional Requirements):

Appearance: devices should be as small as possible in order to stay hidden or at least be kept away from the general population eye

Compliance requirements:

Use encryption to send data: data should be sent without exposing personal data such as location or voices, by protecting it through encryption.

Applicable data structure

Since the hardware is continuously sending information that must be received in a centralized system, the most efficient data structure for processing packets is the Queue. Once it is received, it can be further processed in the same order that it has been received for classification through Big-Data software (Morton & Song, 2007).

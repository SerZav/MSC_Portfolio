One interesting point about this unit was the importance of assumptions. Being a developer, I constantly have a hard time making estimations. Now, I realize that assumptions are a crucial component of estimating. I have been involved in several disagreements while estimating, where a clear explanation about assumptions could have helped toward improving estimations, thus getting a better result. It might be challenging to convince an inexperienced project manager to consider assumptions a valuable estimation aid. Hopefully, if I get the opportunity to become a Project Manager, I will do my best to discuss assumptions with different actors involved.

About SEPM

Urgency can be seen from different points of view. Software engineers, managers and stakeholders will have their own opinions, and can lead to several problems, including panic, bad decisions, bad estimations, rush in requirement gathering, etc.

The project manager is the only responsible for putting together the opinions, estimations and scope of every team involved, for a final scope definition. The first step should be to discuss the scope and create a vision and scope document. Creating and circulating this document through every actor involved, helps sharing a common vision of the project from the start.

Planning

The project plan with define the work that needs to be done and who will do it. There are several tools and techniques that help in project management. For example, the Gantt chart is a graph that includes dates, milestones, progress, and can be used for estimation and project planning.

Estimation

As a rule of thumb, a project can be divided into between 10 and 20 parts or tasks. Depending on the size of the project, those parts will be smaller or bigger. It will normally start with a WBS (Work Breakdown Structure), which is a list of tasks to perform to consider the project as completed. The next step is to estimate the duration for each part (Bodunwa & Makinde, 2020).

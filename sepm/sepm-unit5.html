Content

User experience

It is hard to measure usability on a software because of subjectivity in opinions. What can be good for someone, can be bad for someone else. Developers and customers tend may have different opinions on the outcome of a project if there is not enough communication throughout the project development.

Among the metrics that can be used to measure user experience (UX), we have behavioural and attitudinal. Behavioural include what the user does, measuring views, time on the page, interactions, etc. Attitudinal on the other hand, track what the user says (opinions, reviews, ratings, etc). Loyalty can be measured by asking the user to provide a value (usually between 1 and 10) to indicate if they would recommend the product to families or friends.

Factors Affecting User Experience

In recent years, an unstoppable race for grabbing the customer’s attention helped to develop several concepts to improve the user’s experience. Companies know that price or quality is not enough to secure preference. Gamification, for example, adds concepts and mechanics more typical to games to make users feel like they are playing or competing. These elements can be added regardless of the type of interface, whether it is a business application, a website, or a social network (Berni & Borgianni, 2021).

Another important factor to consider when studying user experience is interrelationship. User-generated content (UGC) includes a different feeling that may appeal to a group of users that want to express themselves through reviews or comments. While UGT is a good way to grab the customer’s attention, it is also a good way to review the interface itself (Yang et al., 2019). UGT can be a review, a picture, a user profile, or any other interaction that can be recorded and reviewed by others.

If we include new trends into the equation such as augmented reality, the approach to study the user experience needs to be completely revised. This scenario considers feelings and sensations in a very different way than when dealing with traditional schemas (Han et al., 2019). However, if we see the components of User Experience by Thüring and Mahlke (2007), we find that it is still a good representation of how this combination of factors can affect the overall judgment and future reuse of this kind of device.

In my opinion, the CUE model is applicable to most situations where no interaction with other users is required. But in a world where most experiences are oriented to help users to express themselves, we need to consider the addition of the expression factor in user experience. It is a component that could be related to emotions, as much as to instrumental and non-instrumental qualities.

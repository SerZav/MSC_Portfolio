Initial Post

The case study I have chosen depicts how economic interests prevailed over ethics. Many user rights have been obscured by tricking them into adhering to services without their knowledge or consent. This technique is known as dark user experience or dark pattern. It involves changing the user interface design to deceive, confuse, hide, or bait users into selection options they did not want or know about (Narayanan et. al, 2020).

The company has violated several principles of the ACM code of conduct. It also violates the Data Protection Act 2018 (Data Protection Act, 2018), which states that data must be used fairly and transparently (Data Protection Act 2018). Additionally, the unfavourable treatment against visually impaired people goes against section 15.1 of the Equality Act (Equality Act 2010, 2010).

The manager and the designer represent a key stakeholder group. It is essential to glimpse how they are unaware of the implications of ethics in their work and how it could impact the company and the customer's reputation.

Reflective activity - Ethics in computing

Being a computer professional working for a Healthcare-related company in Spain, there are obvious regulations and compliance policies to protect the integrity of data. However, as a developer, I always tended to concentrate on design patterns, emerging technologies, code readability and extensibility and other fine-tuning aspects of programming. I felt under the impression that it was not my responsibility to look after the rights of the people who trusted their data in our hands. I could experience the same feeling from other colleagues.

It is no surprise privacy is the main ethical concern in the paper by Stahl et al. (2016). As a relevant stakeholder in my company, especially in the eHealth industry, the data privacy of patients should be a priority. I oversee the integration between new and existing systems. In every design, I must consider the possible threats both internal and external to protect data. From an internal perspective, secure authentication methods and restricted physical access are critical (Hathaliya & Tanwar, 2020). Also, sharing the ethical concerns with every stakeholder involved and keeping track of their awareness of this topic, is crucial for my role.

Another important ethical concern applicable to my role is professionalism. It is extremely related to privacy in many aspects, since acting professionally while trying to accomplish tasks demanded by a customer or a manager, implies also considering privacy and good practices. The best foundation to act professionally and create awareness between team members is to stand by the principles of a code of conduct of a professional body. One difficult situation I might encounter is when colleagues do not know any code of ethics and prefer to favour time over quality or good practices. It is a challenge to follow ethical recommendations when your team do not feel it necessary. Ideally, I would try to explain to the team our responsibilities as computing professionals to the profession and the public.

An additional challenge I could experience in this role is related to management or project direction. They might choose not to comply with ethical recommendations to accomplish time, budget or customer-specific requirements. One measure that I strongly believe would be beneficial in this scenario is to start designing from an ethical perspective (Mulvenna et al., 2017). That is, considering every ethical position from the beginning of every design. It can still be criticized by management, but making it clear that a change that affects ethics will go against professional principles, could derive from a change of direction.
